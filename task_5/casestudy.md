# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI Payment Fraud


What the Crime Is
The crime of UPI fraud is to deceive users into authorizing a debit from their bank accounts. Unlike other hacking crimes, this type of fraud is referred to as "human hacking," where the victim is tricked into entering their UPI PIN under a false premise of receiving money.

How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

The Hook: The scammer calls a victim, pretending to be a representative of a utility service provider or a bank, claiming a refund is due.

The Pressure: The scammer pressures the victim, claiming a refund will expire in a matter of minutes.

The Trap: The scammer sends a "Collect Request" through a UPI-based application, such as Google Pay or PhonePe.

The Error: The victim, believing they must "confirm" they are receiving money, presses on the notification and is required to enter their UPI PIN.

The Theft: Since a PIN is required to send money, the scammer instantly debits money from the victim's account.

Target Audience
Though anyone can fall victim to this type of scam, scammers are most likely to target small online sellers on e-marketplaces and senior citizens who are familiar with UPI but are unaware that receiving money never requires a PIN.

Consequences
The victims will immediately incur a loss, where they will lose their savings within a matter of seconds. The victims will also be unable to recover their money, as they technically "authorized" the transaction, and since a PIN was used, they will only be able to recover a small fraction of their money, typically less than 10%. This will also result in psychological distress, where victims will lose trust in digital banking.
Financial Depletion: The victims will lose their savings within a matter of seconds, as the attacker will typically follow up with various "failed transactions" excuses to drain more money from the victims' accounts.

Recovery Challenges: The victims will be unable to recover their money since they "authorized" the transaction, and since a PIN was used, they will only be able to recover a fraction of their money, typically less than 10%.

Psychological Impact: A loss of trust in digital systems, where victims will be forced to use cash transactions.
